BrainWave Diagnostics Project

A web app utilizing Data Science and Machine Learning to analyze brain scan reports for ADHD detection.

Personas

Persona 1: Sarah Thompson

Age: 38
Occupation: Teacher

Goals: To understand if her child’s inattention and hyperactivity are due to ADHD. She seeks reliable and scientifically backed tools.

Behaviors: Consults healthcare professionals, uses educational apps, concerned about privacy.

Motivations: Ensure her child gets appropriate help if needed while avoiding unnecessary medication.

Persona 2: Dr. David Perez

Age: 45
Occupation: Pediatric Neurologist

Goals: Enhance ADHD diagnosis by integrating brain scan data with machine learning insights.

Behaviors: Utilizes technology in clinic, values scientific accuracy, follows ADHD research advancements.

Motivations: Improve patient outcomes through cutting-edge diagnostic tools.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: Parent Using the App for ADHD Detection

Sarah uploads her child’s brain scan report. The system processes the data and provides an easy-to-understand summary with ADHD markers and recommends a consultation with her healthcare provider.

Scenario 2: Doctor Integrating the Tool into Clinical Practice

Dr. Perez uploads multiple patients' brain scans. The system provides dopamine and norepinephrine transporter binding levels, theta/beta ratios, and functional connectivity metrics. The AI-based summary helps him make accurate ADHD diagnoses efficiently.

Storyboard

Step-by-step interaction for Sarah’s use case:

  1. Sarah opens the app and is prompted to upload brain scan reports.
  2. She selects the reports from her phone and uploads them.
  3. The system analyzes the fMRI, SPECT, and qEEG data.
  4. Sarah receives a simplified report with ADHD likelihood and a suggestion to consult a neurologist.
  5. She downloads or shares the report with her healthcare provider.
