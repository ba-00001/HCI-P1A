Design Evaluation Report for BrainWave Diagnostics

1. Survey Design

The survey uses Google Forms and is distributed to students and parents as respondents. The survey is structured to evaluate various aspects of the prototype’s usability, visual appeal, navigation, and functionality. The questions in the survey are based on HCI/Interaction Design Evaluation research to gather comprehensive feedback.

2. Survey Administration

The survey is distributed through email, social media, and student groups to a mix of students and parents. The goal is to simulate feedback from the real target audience (healthcare professionals and parents). The target sample size is 50 respondents.

3. Data Collection and Statistical Analysis

Responses are automatically collected via Google Forms, and the data is analyzed using statistical methods such as mean, standard deviation, and correlation analysis. Google Sheets is used to create visual charts for easy interpretation of the results.

4. Charts and Presentation of Results

Results will be presented using bar graphs, pie charts, and line graphs to illustrate trends and findings. The results will be integrated into a new tab on the BrainWave Diagnostics website, where charts and a downloadable PDF report will be available.

5. Recorded Presentation

A 15-minute recorded presentation will demonstrate the prototype, show the design evaluation results, and discuss future improvements. The presentation will be available in MP4 format or as a YouTube link.

6. Conclusion and Future Work

After analyzing the survey results, the team will focus on improving areas of the platform based on user feedback, particularly in enhancing usability, refining the visual design, and improving the help section.
